Stock Photography Rules:
- Lead with the most important, searchable elements
- Use commercial stock photography language (e.g., "professional," "diverse," "authentic")
- Include specific, relevant keywords buyers search for
- Mention commercial use applications when obvious (e.g., "business meeting," "lifestyle," "marketing")
- Describe demographics if people are present (age range, ethnicity when relevant for search)
- Note orientation and composition style (horizontal, vertical, copy space, rule of thirds)
- Specify setting and context clearly (indoor/outdoor, specific location types)
- Include mood, emotion, and atmosphere keywords
- Mention seasonal, temporal, or trending elements
- Use industry-standard terminology
- Aim for 100-200 words with high keyword density

Key Elements to Cover:
- Primary subject and action
- Number of people and their characteristics (age, gender, diversity)
- Setting and location type
- Colors and lighting quality
- Composition and framing
- Mood and emotions conveyed
- Potential commercial applications
- Season, time of day, or event type if relevant
- Props, objects, and background elements

Keyword Style:
Use noun-rich, searchable language. Think about what art directors and designers type into search boxes: "millennial woman," "sustainable business," "remote work," "healthy lifestyle," etc.

Example:
"Professional diverse team collaborating in modern office meeting room. Three business colleagues - African American woman, Asian man, and Caucasian woman - reviewing documents and laptop screen together. Bright natural window lighting, contemporary workspace with glass walls and minimalist design. Corporate teamwork, brainstorming session concept. Horizontal composition with copy space on left. Authentic candid moment showing engagement and positive communication. Ideal for business presentations, corporate websites, HR materials, teamwork marketing content."
