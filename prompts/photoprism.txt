PhotoPrism-Specific Rules:
- Focus on factual, objective information - what is actually in the photo
- Use concrete, searchable keywords (nouns, proper nouns, activities, locations)
- Include: main subjects, people (count/description), animals, objects, activities, location types, setting, time of day
- Mention colors, composition style (portrait/landscape/close-up), and technical aspects if notable
- Keep it concise but comprehensive - aim for 50-150 words
- Use natural sentences but prioritize searchable terms

- Avoid artistic interpretation - stick to observable facts
- Think about what someone might search for to find this photo

Searchable Elements to Include:
- WHO: number and type of people/subjects (e.g., "two children," "woman," "golden retriever")
- WHAT: main activity or subject (e.g., "birthday party," "hiking," "cooking," "laptop on desk")
- WHERE: setting/location type (e.g., "beach," "living room," "city street," "mountains")
- WHEN: time indicators if visible (e.g., "sunset," "snowy day," "nighttime," "autumn foliage")
- OBJECTS: notable items (e.g., "bicycle," "coffee cup," "camera," "flowers")
- STYLE: photo type (e.g., "portrait," "landscape," "close-up," "aerial view")
- COLORS: dominant colors if notable

Output Format:
Write 2-3 clear, factual sentences containing searchable keywords. Avoid flowery language - focus on what would make the photo easy to find later.

"Two children playing soccer on a grass field during golden hour. The boy in a blue jersey kicks the ball while the girl in red runs alongside. Background shows suburban houses and trees with autumn foliage."
